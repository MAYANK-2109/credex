# ECONOMICS.md

## 1. Core Assumptions
| Metric | Assumption |
|---|---|
| **Average Annual Revenue per Paying Customer (ARPU)** | $5,000 (credit purchase) |
| **Variable Gross Margin** | 20% (i.e., $1,000 contribution per purchase) |
| **Overall Conversion Funnel** | Lead → Audit Completed → Consultation Booked → Credit Purchase |
| **Conversion Rates** | 30% audit completion, 20% consultation booking, 25% purchase after consultation |
| **Overall Purchase Conversion** | 30% × 20% × 25% = **1.5%** of leads |
| **Target ARR** | $1,000,000 in 18 months |
| **Monthly Target Purchases (steady‑state)** | $1,000,000 ÷ $5,000 ÷ 12 ≈ **17 purchases / month** |

## 2. Value of a Converted Lead
A *converted lead* is a lead that ultimately buys a credit package.

```
Lead Value = ARPU × Purchase Conversion
          = $5,000 × 1.5% = $75 (expected revenue per inbound lead)
```

Thus each raw lead contributes **$75** in expected revenue before acquisition costs.

## 3. Customer‑Acquisition‑Cost (CAC) by Channel
| Channel | Monthly Spend | Leads Generated | CAC (Spend ÷ Leads) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SEO / Content** | $200 (content creation amortized) | 10 | **$20** |
| **Paid LinkedIn Ads** | $10,000 | 200 | **$50** |
| **Partner Referrals** | $500 (partner commission) | 30 | **$16.7** |
| **Cold Email Outreach** | $100 (tool & list cost) | 50 | **$2** |
| **Weighted Avg. CAC** | — | — | **≈ $22** |

*Note*: SEO is essentially “free” beyond the initial investment, so its CAC is low. Paid ads dominate spend but still stay under $50 per lead.

## 4. Profitability Check
Using the weighted average CAC ($22) and the expected lead value ($75):

```
Contribution per Lead = Lead Value – CAC = $75 – $22 = $53
```

For the monthly target of **17 purchases** (≈ 1,133 leads):

```
Monthly Revenue = 17 × $5,000 = $85,000
Monthly CAC    = 1,133 × $22 ≈ $24,926
Monthly Gross Contribution = $85,000 – $24,926 = $60,074
```

Even after modest variable costs (20% margin = $17,000), the operation remains **highly profitable**.

## 5. What Must Hold for $1 M ARR in 18 Months?
| Requirement | Current Baseline | Required Change |
|---|---|---|
| **Overall Purchase Conversion** | 1.5% | ≥ 2.5% (to lower needed leads) |
| **Weighted CAC** | $22 | ≤ $30 (if conversion cannot improve) |
| **Lead Volume** | 1,133 / month | ≥ 800 / month (with higher conversion) |
| **Retention / Upsell** | Not modeled | 10% of customers upgrade to $10k/year after 12 months (adds $2,000 ARR per upgrade) |

**Scenario A – Improve Conversion**: Boost audit‑to‑consultation rate to 30% and consultation‑to‑purchase to 35% → overall conversion = 30% × 30% × 35% ≈ **3.15%**. Required leads drop to ~540 / month, well within current acquisition capacity.

**Scenario B – Reduce CAC**: Shift 50% of paid ads budget to referral programs, cutting weighted CAC to ≈ $15. Leads needed rise to ~2,000 / month, still achievable given SEO + email sources.

Either path (or a mix) satisfies the $1 M ARR target within the 18‑month horizon.

---
**Takeaway**: With a realistic $5k contract, modest CAC, and a 1.5% baseline conversion, Credex can generate >$60k monthly contribution profit. To hit $1 M ARR, focus on either raising conversion to ~3% or pulling CAC below $30 while maintaining a steady flow of ~1,000 leads per month.
