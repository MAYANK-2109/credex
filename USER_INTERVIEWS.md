# USER_INTERVIEWS.md

## Interview 1: Priyanshu Singh

* **Role:** Software Development Engineer (SDE)
* **Company Stage:** Big Tech / Enterprise (Amazon)
* **Duration:** 12 minutes

### Direct Quotes

* "The tool is good [but] Builder needs some more exposure to the topic."
* "Focus on the colors and symmetry."
* "Keep it up."

### The Most Surprising Thing They Said

Coming from an engineer at a major tech company, I expected deep technical feedback on the codebase or architecture. Instead, the most surprising thing was how heavily he focused on the visual presentation—specifically urging me to prioritize UI symmetry and the color palette right out of the gate to give the tool more credibility.

### What It Changed About Our Design

His feedback forced me to rethink our visual hierarchy. I went back to the drawing board to update the **Hero section** entirely, focusing on strict layout symmetry, cleaner alignment, and a more polished, professional color scheme to ensure the interface matches the utility of the tool.

## Interview 2: Divyansh Sharma

* **Role:** Core Coordinator
* **Company Stage:** Student Developer Organization (Turing Club of Programmers, NIT Raipur)
* **Duration:** 18 minutes

### Direct Quotes

* "The overall project is good but [you] need to focus on the responsiveness."
* "Add multiple themes like dark and light."
* "Try to add something that attracts the user, like a very short and fast quick audit or calculator."

### The Most Surprising Thing They Said

I expected a club coordinator to focus on features that serve a community or group dynamic. Surprisingly, he pinpointed a specific user-acquisition hook: a "fast, quick audit or calculator." He emphasized that users have short attention spans and need immediate value before they will commit to exploring the rest of the app.

### What It Changed About Our Design

This interview directly shifted our feature roadmap and UI controls. Based on his critique, I implemented a **Theme Switch button** to instantly toggle between light and dark modes, audited the entire application for mobile responsiveness, and designed a brand-new **Quick Audit section** right on the landing page to hook users immediately.
